Answers:

  1. a) The blue color fades because copper ions are reduced at the cathode during electrolysis, leading to a decrease in copper ions in solution.
    b) Solid sodium chloride does not conduct electricity because the ions are not free to move, while in aqueous solution, ions are free and can carry current.
    c) Copper is a good conductor of electricity, but since it does not dissociate into ions in a solution, it is classified as a non-electrolyte.
    d) Cane sugar solution does not conduct electricity because it does not dissociate into ions, while sodium chloride dissociates into Na⁺ and Cl⁻ ions, which conduct electricity.
  2. d) Lead is deposited at the cathode.
  3. Silver nitrate solution is used because it provides a continuous supply of Ag⁺ ions, while double cyanide of sodium and silver does not release sufficient Ag⁺ ions for electroplating.
  4. The article to be electroplated is placed at the cathode because it needs to gain metal ions, which are reduced at the cathode.
  5. a) At the cathode, copper metal is deposited. At the anode, oxygen gas is released, or the anode dissolves if it's made of copper.
    b) The concentration of copper ions in the electrolyte remains the same.
    c) Reactions:
    Cathode: Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Cu
    Anode: 2H₂O → O₂ + 4H⁺ + 4e⁻ (if an inert anode is used).
  6. a) The cathode reaction: Ni²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Ni (nickel is deposited).
    b) The anode reaction: Ni → Ni²⁺ + 2e⁻ (nickel dissolves).
  7. a) At the anode, copper dissolves.
    b) Reactions:
    Anode: Cu → Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻
    Cathode: Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Cu
    c) The blue color of copper(II) sulfate solution remains unchanged because the concentration of copper ions in the solution remains constant as copper is both deposited at the cathode and dissolved at the anode.
  8. Aluminum is extracted by electrolysis because its oxide is very stable and cannot be reduced by ordinary chemical reducing agents, unlike copper, lead, and iron. Mercury and silver can be extracted by thermal decomposition as their oxides decompose easily when heated.
  9. Calcination is the process of heating an ore in the absence of air to remove volatile impurities.
    Example: ZnCO₃ → ZnO + CO₂.
  10. a) ii) Magnesium
    b) ii) Magnesium oxide
  11. a) The Bayer process
    b) 2Al(OH)₃ → Al₂O₃ + 3H₂O
  12. a)
    i) Al₂O₃ + NaOH → NaAlO₂ + H₂O
    ii) NaAlO₂ + H₂O → Al(OH)₃ + NaOH
    iii) 2Al(OH)₃ → Al₂O₃ + 3H₂O
    b) Cryolite (Na₃AlF₆) is used in molten form to dissolve aluminum oxide.
    c) C (graphite) + O₂ → CO₂
  13. Powdered coke is sprinkled on top of the electrolyte to prevent the loss of heat and reduce oxidation of the molten aluminum.
  14. Zinc is used to cover iron because zinc is more reactive than iron and acts as a sacrificial metal. It oxidizes in place of iron, preventing rusting.
  15. Reasons for alloying:
    i) To increase strength
    ii) To improve corrosion resistance
    iii) To enhance hardness
    iv) To improve electrical or thermal conductivity
