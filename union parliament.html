2) Answer the following questions (2M each)

  1. Why is Rajya Sabha called as the 'Permanent House'?
  2. How are the members of Lok Sabha elected? What is their tenure?
  3. Mention two occasions on which the President addresses a joint session of the Parliament.
  4. Mention any one matter where the Rajya Sabha enjoys equal powers with the Lok Sabha.
  5. How are the members of Rajya Sabha elected?
  6. Mention any two exclusive powers of the Lok Sabha.
  7. Mention any two circumstances under which the seat of a member of parliament becomes vacant.
  8. What are the minimum qualifications a person should have in order to become eligible as a member of Rajya Sabha?
  9. How does the Parliament exercise control over the Executive?

Answers:

  1. The Rajya Sabha is called the "Permanent House" because it never dissolves like the Lok Sabha. Its members serve six-year terms, and every two years, one-third of the members retire, keeping the house always in session.
  2. The members of Lok Sabha are elected directly by the people of India by direct voting. The term of office of a member is 5 years.
  3. President addresses a joint session of the Parliament at:
    • the beginning of the first session after each general election to the Lok Sabha.
    • the beginning of the first session of each year (usually the Budget Session).
  4. The Rajya Sabha enjoys equal powers with Lok Sabha at certain points:
    • Both houses must agree on ordinary bills, and if there is a disagreement, it can be resolved through a joint sitting.
    • Both houses must pass constitutional amendment bills by a two-thirds majority of members present and voting.
    • Both houses must approve proclamations of national emergency, President's rule in a state, and financial emergency.
    • Both houses must discuss matters of national interest, policy, and any issues of public importance.
  5. Members of the Rajya Sabha are elected by the elected members of the State Legislative Assemblies through a system of proportional representation by means of a single transferable vote. Additionally, the President of India can nominate 12 members for their expertise in various fields like arts, literature, science, and social services.
  6. Exclusive powers of the Lok Sabha:
    • Only the Lok Sabha can introduce and pass money bills. The Rajya Sabha can only suggest amendments, which the Lok Sabha may accept or reject.
    • The Lok Sabha has the power to pass a vote of confidence or no confidence against the Council of Ministers. A no-confidence vote, if passed, can lead to the resignation of the government.
    • In case of a deadlock between the houses, the Lok Sabha's will would prevail due to its massive majority.
  7. Circumstances under which the seat of a member of parliament becomes vacant:
    • If the member resigns
    • If the member is absent from all meetings of the House for a period of sixty days without permission
    • If the member is disqualified under any law made by Parliament
    • If the member is expelled from the House
  8. Minimum qualifications for a member of Rajya Sabha:
    • Must be a citizen of India
    • Must be at least 30 years of age
    • Must possess such other qualifications as may be prescribed by Parliament
  9. The Parliament exercises control over the Executive through:
    • Question Hour: Members can ask questions to ministers about their departments
    • No-confidence Motion: Can be moved against the government in the Lok Sabha
    • Committees: Various parliamentary committees scrutinize government activities
    • Budget approval: The government needs parliamentary approval for its financial plans

3) Structured Questions (3M, 3M, 4M)

  1. The Union Legislature of India is not only the lawmaking body, but the center of all democratic process. With reference to the parliament, answer the following questions.

    1. Mention any three circumstances under which the Lok Sabha can make laws on a subject in the State list.
    2. Mention any three financial powers of the Union Parliament.
    3. The Speaker plays a key role in maintaining order and ensuring of the smooth function of Lok Sabha. Mention any four functions of the Speaker to support this statement.

  2. With reference to the powers of the Union Parliament, state the following:

    1. Three of its Legislative Powers
    2. Three of its Financial Powers
    3. Four of its Administrative or Executive Powers

Answers:

    1. Three circumstances under which the Lok Sabha can make laws on a subject in the State list:
      • When a national emergency is declared under Article 352 of the Indian Constitution due to war, external aggression, or armed rebellion, the Parliament (which includes the Lok Sabha) gains the power to legislate on subjects in the State list.
      • If the Rajya Sabha passes a resolution by a two-thirds majority declaring that it is necessary or expedient for Parliament to legislate on a matter in the State list in the national interest.
      • When two or more States are of the opinion that the Parliament should legislate on a subject given in the State list, the Parliament may make an Act on that subject but that would be applicable only to the consenting states.
    2. Three financial powers of the Union Parliament:
      • Approval of Budget: The Parliament has the power to approve the annual Union Budget presented by the government.
      • Supplementary Grants: The Parliament can approve supplementary demands for grants when the government requires additional funds during a financial year.
      • Vote on Account and Permissions for Taxes: The Parliament grants a Vote on Account to the government and has the authority to levy and modify taxes across the country.
    3. Four functions of the Speaker that support maintaining order and ensuring smooth functioning:
      • Presiding over Sessions: The Speaker presides over the meetings and sessions of the Lok Sabha. They maintain decorum, ensure that parliamentary rules and procedures are followed, and decide on points of order raised by members.
      • Maintaining Discipline: The Speaker ensures discipline among members by calling members to order if they disrupt proceedings or violate parliamentary norms. They have the authority to take disciplinary action against members who engage in unruly behavior.
      • Regulating Debate: The Speaker regulates debates by giving opportunities to members to speak, ensuring fair participation, and maintaining the relevance of discussions to the agenda. They also ensure that debates are conducted in a respectful manner.
      • Interpreting Rules: The Speaker interprets the rules of procedure and parliamentary conventions to guide members and clarify procedural matters during debates and discussions. Their rulings on procedural issues are final and binding, contributing to the smooth conduct of parliamentary business.
    1. Three Legislative Powers of the Union Parliament:
      • The Parliament has the exclusive power to legislate on subjects listed in the Union List (List I of the Seventh Schedule of the Constitution), such as defense, foreign affairs, banking, and railways.
      • The Parliament can legislate on matters in the Concurrent List (List III of the Seventh Schedule), where both the Union and State governments have concurrent powers. If there is a conflict between a Union law and a State law on a concurrent subject, the Union law prevails.
      • The Parliament has residual legislative powers to make laws on any matter not enumerated in any of the three lists (Union List, State List, or Concurrent List), known as the residuary powers under Article 248 of the Constitution.
    2. Three Financial Powers of the Union Parliament:
      • The Parliament has the authority to levy taxes such as income tax, corporate tax, customs duties, excise duties, and service tax across India.
      • The Parliament must approve the annual Union Budget presented by the government. This includes approving government expenditures and revenue proposals for the upcoming fiscal year.
      • The Parliament authorizes the government to withdraw money from the Consolidated Fund of India through appropriation bills. These bills specify the amounts and purposes for which funds can be spent by different government departments and agencies.
    3. Four Administrative or Executive Powers of the Union Parliament:
      • The Parliament exercises oversight over the executive branch by holding it accountable through various means such as debates, questions, and parliamentary committees.
      • The Parliament approves international treaties and agreements entered into by the government. Treaties that require legislative measures or expenditure by the government must be approved by the Parliament.
      • The Parliament has the power to declare a national emergency under Article 352 of the Constitution if the security of India or a part thereof is threatened by war, external aggression, or armed rebellion.
      • The Parliament has the power to impeach the President of India under Article 61 and judges of the Supreme Court and High Courts under Article 124 and Article 218 respectively, for violating the Constitution.
