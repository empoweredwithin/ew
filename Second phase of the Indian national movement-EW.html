Second Phase of the Indian National Movement - EW

Answer the following Questions

1) One of Lord Curzon's administrative measures that resulted in a strong resentment from the masses was the Partition of Bengal in 1905. In this context, answer the following questions:
a) What was Lord Curzon's argument in favor of the partition of Bengal? How did the nationalists explain Lord Curzon's motives?
b) How did people react to the partition of Bengal?
c) What was the impact of the anti-partition movement?
2) Explain briefly about the Surat split in 1907.
3) What was the main objective of assertive nationalists? Write any four methods that they adopted to fulfill them.
4) In the second phase of the national movement, there emerged a new and a younger group of leaders to regulate the freedom struggle. In the above context, write:
a) Any three contributions of Bal Gangadhar Tilak.
b) Any three contributions of Bipin Chandra pal.
c) Any three contributions of Lala Lajpat Rai.
5) Write any four causes for the rise of Assertive nationalism in India.

ANSWERS:

1)

a)

  • Lord Curzon, the Viceroy of India, argued that the partition of Bengal in 1905 was necessary for administrative efficiency.
  • He claimed that Bengal was a huge province and need better administration.
  • Curzon proposed to divide Bengal into two parts: Eastern Bengal and Assam, with a Muslim-majority, and Western Bengal, with a Hindu-majority.
  • Indian nationalists believed that the partition was not driven by administrative concerns but by a "divide and rule" policy.
  • They saw it as an attempt to weaken the growing nationalist movement by creating religious divisions between Hindus and Muslims.
  • Nationalists argued that the British aimed to curb the Bengali influence over the national movement.

b)

  • The leaders of the protest movement declared it as a day of national mourning throughout Bengal.
  • it was observed as a day of fasting, with hartals all over Kolkata.
  • People walk barefooted and bathed in the Ganga in the early morning hours.
  • The streets of Kolkata were full of cries of 'Bande Mataram', which became the theme song of the national movement.
  • The ceremony of Raksha Bandhan was celebrated by Hindus and Muslims displaying a symbol of unbreakable unity.

c)

  • Swadeshi and boycott were used as weapons of political agitation for attainment of Swaraj.
  • People lost their faith in the fair play and Justice of the British.
2) The Surat Split ,1907:
  • Differences were growing between the Assertive and Early nationalists.
  • The Assertive nationalists wanted the Swadeshi and Boycott movements to spread national wide, but the early nationalists only wanted them confined to Bengal.
  • There was also a disagreement over the candidature of the next president of the next Congress session.
  • In 1907 at Surat, the assertive nationalists proposed the name of Lala Lajpat Rai as the President of the Surat session, and the early nationalists proposed the name of Rashbehari Gosh.
  • Assertive nationalists were then expelled out of Congress for nine years.
  • The Surat split also weakened the national movement.
3) The main objective of a certain nationalist was the immediate attainment of Swaraj.

The methods adopted by them are:

  • Swadeshi: It means producing necessary items in one's own country and using them for one's use without being dependent on imported goods.
  • Boycott: Economic Boycott of British goods and use of Swadeshi was designed to encourage Indian industries and provide the people with more opportunities for employment.
  • They asked the people to refuse to cooperate with the government and to boycott government services, quotes, schools and colleges.
  • They revived the self-respect by referring to India's past glory like the revival of Shivaji Festival by Tilak.
